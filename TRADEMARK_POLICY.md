## Overview

This document establishes the trademark policy for "DuckBrain" and its associated marks (logos, mascots, wordmarks). While the DuckBrain source code is licensed under the Apache License 2.0, the trademarks are protected separately to maintain brand integrity and prevent confusion.

**The purpose of this policy is to:**
- Protect the DuckBrain brand from misuse
- Provide clarity on acceptable uses
- Enable the community to refer to DuckBrain accurately
- Prevent market confusion between official and unofficial versions

---

## What This Policy Covers

### Trademarks

The following are trademarks of the DuckBrain project:

- **Wordmark:** "DuckBrain" (stylized and unstylized)
- **Logo:** The cybernetic brain with orange duck bill design
- **Mascot:** The cyborg duck scientist character
- **Domain:** duckbrain.dev, duckbrain.io (when registered)
- **Slogan:** "AI Memory System" (when used with DuckBrain branding)

### What Is NOT Covered

This policy does NOT restrict:

- Use of the DuckBrain software under Apache 2.0 license terms
- Forking the codebase for your own projects
- Referring to DuckBrain in documentation, tutorials, or articles
- Using DuckBrain in commercial applications (see license restrictions below)

---

## Permitted Uses

### ✅ You May Use the Trademarks To:

1. **Refer to the official project**
   - "I'm using DuckBrain for my AI memory system"
   - "DuckBrain saved my project hours of development time"

2. **Link to the official repository**
   - "Check out the DuckBrain project at github.com/wojons/duckbrain"

3. **In documentation and tutorials**
   - Screenshot captions: "DuckBrain's Tree view interface"
   - Blog post titles: "Getting Started with DuckBrain"

4. **In forks that don't confuse users**
   - "This is a fork of DuckBrain with added PostgreSQL support"
   - "Based on DuckBrain v1.0, modified for enterprise use"

5. **In academic and research contexts**
   - Citations in papers about memory systems
   - Conference presentations mentioning DuckBrain

### ⚠️ You May NOT Use the Trademarks To:

1. **Imply endorsement**
   - ❌ "Our product is certified by DuckBrain"
   - ❌ "DuckBrain recommends our hosting service"

2. **Create confusingly similar names**
   - ❌ "DuckBrain Pro" (unofficial premium version)
   - ❌ "DuckBrain AI" (confusing fork)
   - ❌ "QuackBrain" (phonetically similar)

3. **Use in commercial contexts without permission**
   - ❌ Selling "DuckBrain Enterprise Edition"
   - ❌ Using the logo on commercial merchandise
   - ❌ Registering "duckbrain.io" for a competing project

4. **Modify the marks**
   - ❌ Changing colors or proportions of the logo
   - ❌ Combining the mascot with unrelated imagery
   - ❌ Creating parody versions that could cause confusion

## Guidelines for Specific Use Cases

### Open Source Forks

If you fork DuckBrain, you must:
- Clearly indicate it's a fork, not the original
- Use a different name that doesn't include "DuckBrain"
- ✅ "MemoryDuck - A DuckBrain fork with enhanced search"
- ❌ "DuckBrain Enhanced" (implies official status)

### Commercial Products

If you build a product using DuckBrain:
- You may mention "Powered by DuckBrain" if accurate
- You may NOT use the DuckBrain logo as your company logo
- You must distinguish your product from DuckBrain itself

### Domain Names

- **Allowed:** duckbrain-docs.io (community documentation)
- **Not Allowed:** duckbrain-official.com (implies official status)

### Merchandise

Community-created merchandise (t-shirts, stickers) is generally allowed if:
- It's non-commercial or low-volume
- It doesn't imply official project endorsement
- It follows the brand guidelines (correct logo usage)

For commercial merchandise production, contact us for permission.

## Brand Guidelines

### Logo Usage

When using the DuckBrain logo (under permitted uses):

- **Do not:**
  - Rotate or flip the logo
  - Change the colors
  - Add effects (drop shadows, gradients not in original)
  - Stretch or compress the proportions

- **Do:**
  - Use the transparent PNG versions provided
  - Maintain aspect ratio
  - Provide adequate clear space around the logo

### Attribution

When mentioning DuckBrain:

```
DuckBrain is a trademark of the DuckBrain project.
Used under the trademark policy at https://github.com/wojons/duckbrain/TRADEMARK_POLICY.md
```

## Enforcement

The DuckBrain project reserves the right to:

- Request cessation of unauthorized trademark use

- Grant exceptions on a case-by-case basis

**We will generally:**
- Issue friendly warnings before taking action
- Work with well-meaning community members to resolve issues
- Focus enforcement on cases of actual confusion or commercial exploitation

## Exceptions

We understand there may be legitimate use cases not covered above. You may request an exception by:

1. Opening an issue with the `trademark-exception` label
2. Describing:
   - What trademark element you want to use
   - How you plan to use it
   - Why the current policy doesn't work for your case
3. We'll respond within 14 business days

**Examples of potential exceptions:**
- Non-profit educational initiatives
- Community conferences and events
- Academic research projects
- Open-source projects that extend (not compete with) DuckBrain
